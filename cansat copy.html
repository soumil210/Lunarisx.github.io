The Mission

The CanSat competition is an international educational program organized by ESERO in collaboration with the European Space Agency (ESA). Students are challenged to design, build, and launch a satellite the size of a soda can, simulating a real space mission and gaining hands-on experience in aerospace engineering.

Team Lunaris-X, from Keizer Karel College in Amstelveen, is developing a fully functional CanSat that performs environmental measurements, transmits flight data to a ground station, and enables in-depth post-flight analysis.

Mission Objectives

Primary Mission

Measure air temperature and air pressure continuously throughout the flight. The collected data is transmitted to the ground station during descent and stored locally as a fail-safe. Pressure data is converted into altitude, allowing reconstruction of the full flight profile and generation of detailed graphs.

Secondary Mission

Record the CanSat’s orientation, rotation, and stability using a 9-axis IMU consisting of an accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer. By combining this data with altitude measurements, a 3D model of the CanSat’s trajectory and attitude is reconstructed, resulting in a realistic visualization of the flight dynamics during ascent, release, descent, and landing.

Why This Mission?

Engineering Relevance

Stability and orientation control are fundamental aspects of all aerospace systems, from sounding rockets to satellites. Measuring real-world motion allows evaluation of aerodynamic behavior and structural design.

Data Visualization

The 3D reconstruction transforms raw sensor data into a clear and intuitive visualization, making otherwise invisible dynamics such as rotation and vibration easier to understand and analyze.
